# Regional Support Consolidation: Proceed, With Sequencing That Protects Quality

**Recommendation:** Consolidate the three regional support teams into a single global function over two quarters. Sequence the APAC region last to protect the highest-CSAT market during transition.

The case for consolidation is strong on cost and on capability. The three teams currently operate with separate tooling stacks, separate escalation paths, and separate knowledge bases that diverge over time. A customer who contacts support from Singapore gets a materially different experience from one who contacts from Amsterdam - not because the product differs, but because the institutional knowledge is siloed. Consolidation addresses both the cost redundancy and the quality inconsistency simultaneously.

**The framework in use here is span of control and knowledge flow.** The question is not whether to consolidate - the analysis supports that clearly - but whether to consolidate all at once or in sequence. A simultaneous consolidation compresses the organizational disruption into a shorter window but creates a larger surface area for quality degradation during transition. A phased approach extends the timeline by approximately six weeks but allows lessons from the first consolidation to be applied to the second and third.

**On the sequencing.** EMEA and North America have comparable CSAT scores and similar ticket volumes. Starting with those two regions and then consolidating APAC provides a meaningful buffer for the highest-performing market. Concerns have been raised about whether the APAC team's local language coverage will be preserved in a consolidated structure - those concerns are valid, and the transition plan should treat language routing as a day-one requirement, not a post-launch optimization.

**What the organization should not do** is treat this as a pure headcount reduction. The efficiency gain from shared tooling and a unified knowledge base is real and durable; the risk is scoping it narrowly as a cost exercise and underfunding the knowledge-base migration. That produces a structure that looks consolidated but operates fragmented.

**The ask.** Approve the two-quarter consolidation plan with APAC sequenced last, fund the knowledge-base migration as a first-class workstream, and schedule a quality review at the six-week mark after each phase closes.
