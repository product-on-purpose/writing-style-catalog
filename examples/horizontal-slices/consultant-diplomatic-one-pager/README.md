# Recipe: consultant-diplomatic-one-pager

A composition of `senior-consultant` voice, `diplomatic` tone, `executive-summary` style, and `one-pager` format. Used for advisory briefs that carry a firm recommendation to a decision-maker who may have conflicting interests at stake.

## When to use

You are advising a senior stakeholder on a binary or multi-option decision where the right call is not politically neutral. The reader has skin in the game, the recommendation may require them to abandon a position they have publicly held, and you need them to act rather than deliberate. This recipe is the right tool when the situation calls for a recommendation that is clear and firm but delivered in a way that gives the reader room to move without losing face. It fits make-or-buy decisions, consolidation proposals, vendor selections, and reorganization recommendations where the analysis is complete and the job of the document is to close the decision.

## When to use something else

If the decision is genuinely open and the reader needs to weigh in before a direction is chosen, use a `problem-solution` style in a less formal format - the executive summary structure presupposes a recommendation exists. If the audience is technical and the decision affects implementation more than strategy, `pragmatic-architect` voice in `decision-log` style will carry more credibility. If the relationship is close and the tone can be direct without diplomatic packaging, `candid` tone in a short memo will read faster and land harder.

## Composition

| Axis | Entry | Why |
|------|-------|-----|
| Voice | `senior-consultant` | Leads with diagnosis, names the framework in use, and treats the reader as a peer who can handle complexity. The voice that earns trust from a skeptical executive. |
| Tone | `diplomatic` | The reader may have a prior position the recommendation contradicts. Diplomatic tone holds the recommendation firm while giving the reader a face-saving path forward. |
| Style | `executive-summary` | Opens with the conclusion. Every paragraph after adds new information; nothing restates. A reader who stops after paragraph one still has what they need to act. |
| Format | `one-pager` | Forces discipline. Situation, central point, implications, and a specific ask - no room for hedging through verbosity. Built for a decision-maker with limited time. |
