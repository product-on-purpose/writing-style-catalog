Subject: I finally understand what you did in that Meridian kickoff

Hi Dana,

I have been meaning to write this for ten years. Something happened last week that finally made me sit down.

Ten years ago, when Marco stepped down two weeks before launch prep, you put my name forward for the Meridian content migration. I had been on the team for nineteen months. I had never managed a timeline, never walked a room full of stakeholders through a project plan as the person responsible. I remember the morning of the first kickoff call - I had the deck open on one screen and the client list on another, going over every name so I would not have to look anyone up while they were watching me.

You stayed. You sat one chair back from the table, and you never said a word. When a stakeholder asked me about the content inventory handoff schedule in week two and I went quiet for what felt like two full minutes, you waited. I found the answer. You had already done your job.

That project closed on time. I was too relieved to understand what had actually happened.

Last Tuesday I put Priya - a strategist on my team, eighteen months in - forward for the Lakeshore rebuild. My director asked if she was ready. I said she was ready enough. I blocked three hours a week for the first month and sat one chair back through her first two stakeholder calls. She ran the third one alone.

I finally understand why I never noticed what you were doing. You made it look like nothing.

Thank you.

Leila
