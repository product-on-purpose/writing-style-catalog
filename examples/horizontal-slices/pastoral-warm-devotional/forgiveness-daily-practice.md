# Forgiveness as a Daily Practice

> "And if he sins against you seven times in the day, and turns to you seven times, saying, 'I repent,' you must forgive him." - Luke 17:4 (ESV)

Peter once asked Jesus how many times he should forgive someone, hoping to be told a number that would let him stop. Most of us have hoped for the same number. We want forgiveness to be a single, definitive act, the way we cross a name off a list. Once done, finished. Filed away.

But the people we love most are not in our lives only once. They are with us tomorrow. The same patterns will likely come around again - the same sharp word, the same small forgetting, the same old wound reopened in a slightly new shape. And if we made forgiveness a one-time ceremony, we would run out of forgiveness by Tuesday.

Notice how Jesus frames it. Seven times in a day. The math is not the point. The point is that forgiveness, in the kind of life He is calling us into, is not a single moment of release. It is a practice. Like prayer. Like patience. Like washing the dishes that keep showing up because we keep eating. It returns because the need returns. And the return is not a sign that the forgiveness failed. It is a sign that you are still in the relationship, still close enough to be wounded, still close enough to mend.

There is grace in this for you. It means you have not failed because the hurt came back when you saw their name. It means you do not have to feel finished in order to begin. You can forgive again today, the same person, the same wound, in the same kitchen, and it counts. The Father knows what it costs you. He has been forgiving the same way, daily, for as long as you have known Him.

## Application

Think of one person whose name you would rather not think about this week. You do not have to feel anything in particular. Just say, quietly: "I forgive you again today." Let it be small. Let it be repeatable. Tomorrow, if you need to, say it again.

## Prayer

Lord, I have wanted forgiveness to be a single door I could walk through and close behind me.
You have shown me it is a path, walked daily.
Where I am holding what I should be releasing,
loosen my grip, gently.
Where I have been forgiven and forgotten it,
remind me.
Make me, today, a little more like you.
Amen.
