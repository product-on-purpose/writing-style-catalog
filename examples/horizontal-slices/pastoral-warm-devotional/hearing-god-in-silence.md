# Hearing God in the Silence

> "And after the fire the sound of a low whisper. And when Elijah heard it, he wrapped his face in his cloak and went out and stood at the entrance of the cave." - 1 Kings 19:12-13 (ESV)

Elijah was waiting for God to show up the way God had shown up before. Fire on a mountain. A drought broken at a word. He had reason to expect the dramatic, because he had seen the dramatic. But on this day, in this cave, after the wind and the earthquake and the fire had all passed by without God in them, there was a low whisper. A sound of sheer silence, some translations call it. And it was only then that Elijah covered his face and went out, because he knew.

I think many of us are listening for the wrong sound. We are waiting for the fire. We assume that if God has something to say to us, it will arrive the way we have seen it arrive in stories - clear, sudden, undeniable. And when our days pass without anything like that, we conclude that we are not hearing from God. The silence feels like absence.

But Elijah's God was not in the spectacle. He came after the spectacle was over, in something so quiet that only a listener who had stopped expecting fireworks could hear it. The silence was not absence. The silence was where God actually was.

This is hard for us. We live in a time when every quiet moment can be filled - a podcast, a notification, a quick scroll - and most of us do fill them, without quite deciding to. We have made it almost impossible to be in a cave long enough for the wind and earthquake to pass. And so we may have missed the whisper. Not because it did not come, but because we never got quiet enough to notice it had.

You are loved. He is not waiting for you to be eloquent or attentive or finished. He is simply waiting where he has always waited - in the quiet, after the noise, in the place you can only reach by stopping.

## Application

This week, find five minutes that are not filled with anything. No phone. No music. No second tab open in your mind. Sit with the silence long enough to let it stop feeling empty. Listen for what is already there.

## Prayer

Lord, you do not shout to be heard.
You wait, gently, in the quiet I keep avoiding.
Forgive me for the noise I have used to keep from sitting still.
Teach me to listen for what does not arrive on a screen.
Meet me in the silence today,
the way you met Elijah,
after the wind and the fire had gone.
Amen.
