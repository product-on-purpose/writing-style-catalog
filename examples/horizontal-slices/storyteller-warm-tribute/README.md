# Recipe: storyteller-warm-tribute

A composition of `storyteller` voice, `warm` tone, `chronological-narrative` style, and `blog-post-long-form` format. Used for public tributes that honor a person by telling their story, not by listing their accomplishments.

## When to use

Someone is leaving, retiring, or being recognized, and you want to mark it for an audience - a team, a company, a community. This recipe fits when the person's impact is best understood as an arc over time, and when concrete scenes will move readers more than a bulleted list of contributions. It is the right tool for retirements, send-offs, and milestone recognitions where the goal is for readers to feel the person's absence and their worth.

## When to use something else

If the tribute is a private message to the person themselves, a `caregiver` / `warm` `email` is more intimate and appropriate. If you are marking a team or product achievement rather than a person, a `storyteller` / `celebratory` announcement keeps the focus on the win. If the occasion is solemn rather than warm - a memorial - a `reverent` tone in a `devotional-entry` carries the weight better.

## Composition

| Axis | Entry | Why |
|------|-------|-----|
| Voice | `storyteller` | Builds the tribute from scenes and specifics. Shows the person through what they did, letting readers draw the conclusion rather than asserting it. |
| Tone | `warm` | Affectionate and human without tipping into eulogy. The register of a colleague who knew the person, addressing others who did too. |
| Style | `chronological-narrative` | Walks the arc in time - early days, a defining moment, the long middle, the send-off - so a whole career reads as a single story. |
| Format | `blog-post-long-form` | A title and room to breathe. The container for a piece meant to be read by many and to give the moment its due length. |
