Subject: Your startup question - a few things I'd want to sit with first

Hi Sam,

Before I offer anything that might be useful, I want to ask you something: do you know what this decision is actually about for you?

Not the job. Not the salary comparison or the equity math. I mean the thing underneath it - the part that made you reach out rather than just run the numbers and move on.

Here's where I'd start: what does "stable" mean to you in this role? Is it the work itself, or the financial floor, or the sense of being known and trusted somewhere? Each of those has a different shape in a startup - or doesn't have a shape there at all.

Then, and I find this one more revealing: if you imagine yourself three years from now and the startup has gone badly, what specifically are you afraid you'll have lost? Not the job - deeper than that. And when you imagine it has gone well, what does "well" actually look like? Not the outcome, but how you're spending your days.

The question underneath both of those: are you optimizing for what you might gain, or trying to avoid a particular kind of regret? Neither answer is wrong. But they point toward different decisions.

I've watched how you work through things, Sam. You tend to locate the real question before most people notice there's more than one question in the room.

So, when you sit with all of that, what does this decision feel like it's actually about?

I think you're closer to your answer than you realize.

Marcus
