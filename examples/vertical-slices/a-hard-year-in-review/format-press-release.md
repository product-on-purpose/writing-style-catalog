Delgado Sets February Date for Meridian Retrospective

Independent consultant confirms timeline for a full written account of the Meridian Community Broadband Initiative's closure, to be delivered directly to the coalition that built it.

COLUMBUS, Ohio, December 31, 2025 - Marcus Delgado, the independent consultant who led the Meridian Community Broadband Initiative from September 2023 until its closure in March, today confirmed that a complete written retrospective for the initiative's eleven-member coalition will be delivered by the end of February. The retrospective will be sent directly to coalition members and will not be published as a public release.

The Meridian Community Broadband Initiative was a volunteer-led coalition drawn from local library systems, a school district technology office, neighborhood associations, and regional internet providers, formed in September 2023 to plan and propose community broadband infrastructure for underserved parts of the region. The coalition produced an infrastructure proposal that held funder and community confidence for the full eighteen-month engagement. The initiative closed in March 2025 when its primary funder withdrew support before the proposal reached deployment.

Delgado said the retrospective will describe the coalition's proposal work, the internal decision-making that shaped it, and what he would approach differently in a future engagement of similar scope.

"A coalition of eleven people gave eighteen months to this proposal, and they are owed a complete account of how it ended, not a summary," said Marcus Delgado, independent consultant and former lead of the Meridian Community Broadband Initiative. "That account is coming to them directly in February. This statement is not a substitute for it."

"Marcus told the eleven of us directly when the funder pulled out, and he was clear the decision wasn't a verdict on the work we did," said Grace Halloran, Branch Manager, Norwood Public Library, and a member of the Meridian coalition. "A full written account was always going to take time to do honestly, and I would rather he take the time than rush it."

Delgado said he is continuing to advise public-sector and nonprofit clients on operations, program delivery, and technology adoption through his independent practice, and remains open to leading future community coalitions of similar scope.

About Marcus Delgado
Marcus Delgado is an independent consultant based in Columbus, Ohio, advising public-sector and nonprofit clients on operations, program delivery, and technology adoption. He has run his own practice since 2019, following six years as a program manager at Aldergate Infrastructure Partners. From September 2023 to March 2025, he led the Meridian Community Broadband Initiative as a volunteer, directing an eleven-person coalition through an eighteen-month effort to plan and propose community broadband infrastructure.
