Dear Hiring Committee,

I am writing to apply for the Director of Coalition Programs role at Fenwick Regional Partnership. I have led a regional infrastructure coalition through its complete lifecycle, funder relationship and closure included, and that full range is what your posting is asking for.

From September 2023 to March 2025, I led the Meridian Community Broadband Initiative, directing eleven volunteer contributors through the planning and proposal stages of a regional broadband effort. The coalition produced a technically sound infrastructure proposal and held both coalition and funder confidence for the full eighteen months of the engagement, the kind of durability your posting is asking for when it calls for a lead who can sustain a coalition through funding uncertainty, not just start one. When Meridian's primary funder withdrew support in March 2025, ending the initiative without a deployment, I managed the closure myself: wound down the coalition's commitments, kept the exit organized, and made sure the decision landed as what it was, a funder's call made above the coalition, not a verdict on the group's work.

I also want to name where I fell short, because it is a fair question and I would rather answer it than leave it open. The first signal that our funder's priorities were shifting reached me four weeks before the closure, and I chose to hold the coalition together with optimism instead of escalating the harder read I already had. I have corrected that since. The standard I now hold myself to is putting a hard signal in front of the people it affects on the timeline the facts require, not the easiest one, and that discipline is what I would bring to Fenwick's coalitions.

I would welcome the opportunity to talk through how that experience applies to Fenwick's coalitions in an interview.

Sincerely,
Marcus Delgado
