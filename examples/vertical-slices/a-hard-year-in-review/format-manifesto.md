# The Early Word: A Manifesto

In March, the Meridian initiative dissolved when the funder that had backed it walked away, and eleven people who had given eighteen months of volunteer work got a thank-you that did not come close to matching what they were owed. By August, Celeste and I had stopped speaking, after six years of closeness had drifted into a silence I spent the spring calling something gentler than what it actually was. A message from Theo has sat unanswered since April. Three different relationships were owed an early word this year, and in every case I delivered a late one, or none at all.

I do not believe anymore that managing how a hard thing looks is the same as managing the hard thing. I believed some version of that for most of this year, and it cost me a coalition's trust, six years of ordinary friendship maintenance I let lapse, and eight months of a message I was fully capable of answering the week it arrived. I am done confusing the story I tell about a situation with the situation itself.

## What I believe

1. A hard truth belongs on the timeline the facts set, not the timeline that is easiest to deliver. Waiting for a better moment to say a true thing is a way of hoping the thing stops being true before I have to say it.
2. Optimism that requires withholding a material fact from the people who trusted me is not optimism. It is a story I am telling myself first and everyone else second.
3. Giving someone space and avoiding a conversation I do not want to have are not the same behavior, and I do not get to use the name of the kinder one to cover for the other.
4. What a commitment costs when it fails is not proof the commitment was a mistake. It is only proof of what full commitment costs, and those are two different findings. This year I nearly let myself believe the second was the first.
5. Silence is a decision. It does not stop being a decision just because it did not feel like one while it was happening.
6. Naming what I do not understand about a loss is more honest than manufacturing a lesson I have not earned. Not every hard year converts into a growth story, and I no longer need mine to.

## What I stand against

I stand against narrative management dressed up as loyalty - the instinct that kept a coalition of eleven aligned around optimism when some of them needed a harder truth weeks before I gave it to them. I stand against calling distance independence, because independence sounds like a choice I made on purpose and distance sounds like what it actually was. I stand against letting an unanswered message get harder to answer with every week I leave it, and then treating the difficulty as a reason instead of the symptom it is. I stand against reframing a genuinely bad year into a tidy account of hidden gifts. Some of what happened this year was simply a loss, and I do not owe anyone, including myself, a silver lining before I am allowed to move forward.

## What I stand for

I stand for delivering the hard version of an update on the date the facts require it, even when a softer version is available and no one would catch the difference right away. I stand for telling the people who have trusted me with their time what I actually know, before I know how the story ends. I stand for full commitment to the next project and the next relationship, on the same terms as before, because this year is evidence of what full commitment can cost and not evidence that it should be rationed going forward. I stand for a written account, not a summary, for anyone who gave real time to something that did not work out. I stand for answering the message before it is comfortable to answer, not after.

## The call

Theo gets an answer in January. Whatever discomfort that conversation holds is smaller than nine months of silence has already cost. The Meridian coalition gets the complete account in February - what happened, what the decision-making looked like from the inside, what I would do differently - not a paragraph and a lessons-learned bullet, because eleven people do not owe me the easy version of their own year. I am not starting the next large initiative until both of those are finished and at least one relationship I let go under-maintained has been actually repaired, not just intended to be.

If you are one of the people who might work with me again, or might trust me with something that matters to you, here is what I am asking in return. Tell me the hard thing early, before I make it easy for you not to. Do not let me manage you with a version of events I have not fully told myself yet. Hold me to the two dates above, and to every date after them. I would rather hear that I am repeating this pattern than be allowed to repeat it quietly. That is the whole of what I am asking anyone to give me for what I am committing to here.
