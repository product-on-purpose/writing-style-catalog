There are years that ask more of you than you agreed to give. This was one of them.

The project - I had been calling it Meridian, a word I chose quickly and came to take seriously - occupied two years of careful work before it ended. It ended without the outcome I had given myself to. Not because the effort was careless. But because the conditions I had built upon did not hold, and I had not seen clearly enough, early enough, that they were shifting. That is a hard thing to say plainly without softening it. I am trying not to soften it.

And then there was the friendship with Elena - which is perhaps too small a word for what it was - that changed in a way I did not choose and could not stop. We did not end badly. We ended with a quietness that was, in its own way, more difficult than argument would have been. What had been close became careful. What had been easy became occasional. I do not know how much of that was mine to have prevented. I suspect some of it was.

A year like this one does not resolve. It does not offer the satisfaction of a clear moral or a changed perspective that redeems the difficulty. Some of what I lost simply stayed lost. What I am choosing to carry forward - not because the loss was secretly worthwhile, but in spite of the fact that it was not - is a more honest accounting of what I am willing to risk, and what I need not promise lightly. That is small. It may be enough.
