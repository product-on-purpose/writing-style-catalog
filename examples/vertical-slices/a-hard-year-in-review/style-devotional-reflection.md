There is a notebook on my desk - one of those thick ones with the elastic band across the cover. I bought it at the start of the year for a project I was certain would work. The name "Aldercroft Initiative" is written inside the front cover in my own hand, and I keep meaning to put it away somewhere. I have not put it away.

That project ran for nine months. Marcus, who led alongside me, worked in a way I have rarely experienced with another person - finishing each other's thinking, catching each other's mistakes, caring about the same things. By November, the people who had commissioned the work decided to discontinue it. Not because the work was bad. Because the conditions had changed.

That is the kind of ending that leaves you with nothing particular to argue against.

What I did not expect was how loss compounds. By the time the project ended, Marcus and I had also grown distant in a way I cannot fully account for. I want to assign fault, and some of it is mine - I became absorbed, I made assumptions, I let silences go longer than I should have. But I cannot draw a clean line. I do not think clean lines are usually available.

There is an old prayer that speaks of asking for "the grace to see clearly what is, and the courage to stop calling it something else." I have been sitting with both of those tasks, and I am not done with either. The year was hard. The relationships it touched were real. The grief is real too.

I wanted, by now, to be able to say: this is what the year meant. This is what it taught me. The loss was preparation for something. But I do not believe that right now. And I think it matters not to pretend.

What I am choosing to carry forward is harder to name. Not a lesson. More like a posture - a decision to let what mattered, matter, without needing it to have earned its cost in some way I can calculate. I gave myself to something that genuinely mattered. That it ended badly does not make it false. It was real. I was present for it.

I wonder if you have been in a year like this. Whether you, too, are sitting with something that did not resolve - something you cannot quite put away. I do not have easy comfort to offer. But I notice I still have the notebook. And I notice I have not crossed the name out.
