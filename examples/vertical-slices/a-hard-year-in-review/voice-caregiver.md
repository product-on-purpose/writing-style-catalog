The year needs naming, not summarizing. The Fernwood project closed in October - three years of work, a grant that ran out, and a decision made by others who did not have to live inside it. I sat at the kitchen table the morning after and ate toast I did not taste. That was how it went.

Around the same time, the friendship with Elena became something else. Not ended, exactly, but changed in a way I did not get to choose. The last real conversation we had was in March, in her car in a parking lot, and neither of us knew then that it was the last one. I have replayed that conversation enough times now that I am not sure I am remembering it correctly.

Those two things happened in the same year. They did not balance each other out. They did not have a lesson hiding inside them. They were just two hard things that asked a lot.

What they asked: that I sleep more than I wanted to, because without sleep I became someone I did not want to be. That I eat actual meals even when I was not hungry. That I cancel some things I had said yes to, and not apologize more than once.

What I got wrong: I waited too long to ask for help, in both cases. I thought that staying busy was the same as staying well. It is not.

What I am carrying forward, not because the year was secretly good but because I have to carry something: I know what a real next step feels like now. Not a plan, not a recovery arc. Just the next thing, small enough to actually do.

This part was hard. That is allowed to be the whole sentence.
