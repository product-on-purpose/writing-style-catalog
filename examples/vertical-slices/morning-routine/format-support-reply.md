Hi Elena,

You flagged in the repo discussion that the bodyweight sequence in `protocol/movement.md` is aggravating your knee, and that you have been skipping the movement step outright for the past two weeks instead of logging a modified version. That is a legitimate reason to change the step, not a compliance problem.

The file lists walk, stretch, or bodyweight work as examples, not a requirement. The actual rule I wrote down for that module is "heart rate up, not crushing" - the exercise itself is yours to choose. Two swaps that meet the rule without loading the knee:

1. A brisk walk on its own, no bodyweight work attached. Ten to fifteen minutes clears the module.
2. Seated upper-body strength (rows, presses) on mornings when walking is not realistic either.

Either one satisfies the step as written. Update your fork's log to mark those mornings completed, not partial, once you are running a swap - the module exists to move you out of stillness, not to enforce a specific exercise, so a knee-safe substitute is not a smaller version of the protocol. It is your version of it.

Let me know if the swap does not hold up over the next couple of weeks, or if the knee changes how you sequence light and planning too.
