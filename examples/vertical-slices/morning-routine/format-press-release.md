**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**Jordan Voss Completes Month One of Public Morning-Routine Experiment**

*Month One closes with 23 of 30 mornings finished; Month Two adds a travel variant and a Sunday planning session*

DENVER, Colo., June 14, 2026 - Jordan Voss, a Program Manager at Aldergate Health Network, completed Month One of a personal, public morning-routine experiment today and moved directly into Month Two. Twenty-three of 30 mornings were finished against the full four-step protocol, with the hardest rule, phone deferred until the final step, held on 28 of 30. Month Two adds two changes drawn from Month One's own data: a travel variant and a Sunday-evening planning session.

The protocol, now in its third revision, runs the same sequence each weekday morning: water on waking, ten minutes of light, fifteen minutes of movement, and ten minutes of paper planning, before the phone leaves the kitchen. Month One's daily log, published as a single row per morning in the project's public repository, recorded a 76.7 percent full-completion rate and a 63.3 percent hold rate on the 6:15 a.m. wake target. "Steady" was the most common single-word mood entry, ahead of "tired," "sharp," and "anxious."

Two failure patterns, drawn from the log rather than assumed, shaped Month Two's design. Tuesdays accounted for three of Month One's four unexplained misses, and two of three travel days failed because the protocol assumed a kitchen and a nightstand a hotel room does not provide. Month Two adds a 15-minute Sunday planning session, a written travel variant for each step, and an explicit weekend rule; the core sequence does not change.

"Twenty-three out of thirty is not a perfect record, and I did not design this to produce one," said Jordan Voss, creator of morning-experiment. "The number I trust is the twenty-eight out of thirty on keeping the phone in the kitchen until planning was done. That rule carried more of the outcome than the other three steps combined, and Month Two does not touch it."

"Nobody was checking this, and there was no team depending on it," said Priya Nakamura, Director, Care Access Programs, Aldergate Health Network, who has supervised Jordan for five years. "Jordan measured it honestly anyway and reported the mornings that did not work. That is the same discipline I see in how they run programs for us."

Month Two runs for the next 30 days. The next status report is scheduled for day 60, on or around July 13, 2026, in the same repository that has carried the daily log without a gap since Month One began.

About morning-experiment
morning-experiment is Jordan Voss's public, version-controlled repository documenting a self-directed attempt to redesign the first hour after waking. The project publishes its protocol, daily completion log, and weekly retrospectives, including the mornings that did not hold. It is open for anyone to fork; readers who run their own version are invited to open a discussion and report back what changed.
