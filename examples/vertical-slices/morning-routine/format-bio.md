Jordan Voss is a Program Manager at Aldergate Health Network. They coordinate delivery across concurrent care-access initiatives there, and introduced the decision-record template the team now uses to keep scope changes from being re-litigated after the fact. In May 2026, they pointed that same instinct at their own mornings: a written decision record laid out a four-module protocol - water, light, movement, paper planning - before the trial's first day, and a version-controlled repository logged wake time, completion, and mood every day for thirty days. The result was 23 of 30 mornings completed in full and phone use deferred until the planning step on 28 of 30 days, tracked in public and written up as weekly retrospectives rather than kept as a private resolution.

Jordan is based in Denver and is currently running Month Two of the protocol, with a travel variant in testing and a controlled one-week removal of the light step scoped to find out which parts of the routine are actually load-bearing.
