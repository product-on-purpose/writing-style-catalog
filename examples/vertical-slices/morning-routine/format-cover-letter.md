Dear Hiring Team,

I'm applying for the Senior Program Manager role. For the past five years I've owned cross-functional delivery for organizations that needed one person to hold scope, timeline, and stakeholder alignment across initiatives with no natural owner - most recently at Aldergate Health Network. Your posting asks for someone who documents a decision, executes it, and revises it against evidence rather than instinct. That is the exact shape of how I already work.

The clearest example of that isn't in my job history. It's a personal project I started in May: I wanted to fix a reactive first hour of the day, and instead of just trying harder, I wrote a one-page decision record before I changed anything - the four-step protocol, the alternatives I rejected and why, and a review date. I built a version-controlled log to track it daily. By day 30 I had completed the full protocol on 23 of 30 mornings (76.7 percent) and held the hardest single rule, phone out of reach until planning was done, on 28 of 30 (93.3 percent). I did not hit every target. I said so in the written retrospective, then used the log to identify Tuesdays and travel days as the actual failure points instead of guessing. That is the sequence I would bring to this role: define the decision in writing, execute it, measure it honestly, and change only what the evidence says to change.

The same discipline shows up in the paid work. At Aldergate I run quarterly planning between clinical operations and IT, two groups with different priorities and no shared calendar, and convert whatever they agree on into a backlog engineering can actually commit to. Before that, at Brindle Municipal Services, I coordinated input sessions ahead of two capital-improvement votes, where getting the stakeholder sequencing right was a public deadline, not a private one. If the personal project answers whether I can hold myself to a decision, these two answer whether I can hold a room full of people who do not report to me to one.

I would welcome the opportunity to discuss the Senior Program Manager role and how both the last five years and the last month would apply to it. I'm available for a call this week or next, whenever works on your end.

Sincerely,
Jordan Voss
