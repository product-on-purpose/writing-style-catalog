Dear Ms. Okafor,

I am writing to apply for the Director of People Operations role at Wrenfield Analytics. Your posting names a stalled, unresolved return-to-office decision as the role's first priority, and that is precisely the work I have already done: I took an undefined "flexible" policy and turned it into a ratified position that both sides of the debate could live with.

At Brackenridge Group, I chartered and chaired a cross-functional working group across HR, Facilities, Engineering, and Legal to replace that ambiguity with a real decision. We did not split the difference between full in-office and fully remote. We designed a structured hybrid model, now ADR-0012: two mandatory anchor days, Tuesday and Thursday, for shared collaboration, and three fully flexible days for everything else. I wrote the case for it in Position Brief v2 and put formal answers in writing to the two objections that mattered most before either side had to raise them again: the office-first argument that trust and spontaneous collaboration require daily proximity, and the remote-only argument that any mandated presence narrows the talent pool and burdens caregivers, people outside commuting range, and people with disabilities.

A ratified position is not the same as an executed one, and your posting also asks for someone who can hold cross-departmental ownership after the writing is done. I resolved a decision-ownership conflict between Facilities and HR at Brackenridge, aligning our room-booking policy to the anchor-day schedule ahead of the company-wide announcement, because a well-argued position nobody has the authority to execute is not yet a policy.

I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how this experience applies directly to Wrenfield's decision - please let me know a convenient time for an interview.

Sincerely,
