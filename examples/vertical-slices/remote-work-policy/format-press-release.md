**Millbrook Software Adopts Two-Day Hybrid Policy, Shares Framework**

*Two mandatory office days a week, three fully flexible - and the underlying model is free for other employers to adopt.*

COLUMBUS, Ohio, July 14, 2026 - Millbrook Software announced today that it has adopted a structured hybrid work-location policy requiring two shared office days each week, Tuesday and Thursday, while keeping the remaining three days fully flexible for employees who can do their jobs remotely. The policy takes effect in August and applies to every office-eligible role. Millbrook is also publishing the framework behind the policy, called hybrid-anchor, under an open license so other employers can use or adapt it.

Under the policy, employees in roles that require office access are expected on site every Tuesday and Thursday. The other three weekdays are fully flexible, and employees do not need manager approval to work from home on those days. Employees hired into remote-eligible roles are not subject to the anchor-day requirement, and any other exception requires documentation reviewed by an employee's manager and the people team.

The policy follows a months-long internal review led by a cross-functional working group, which weighed the strongest arguments on both sides of the return-to-office debate: that in-person time speeds trust-building for newer hires, and that location mandates shrink the talent pool and disrupt employees who built their lives around remote flexibility. Rather than split the difference, the working group designed anchor days as shared infrastructure for collaboration and left the remaining schedule flexible by default.

"We didn't want to pick a side in the return-to-office debate, because both sides are naming real costs," said Dana Okonkwo, Chief People Officer, Millbrook Software. "Two anchor days is enough to rebuild the kind of trust that erodes when people never share a room. Three flexible days is enough to keep the people who joined us because the job didn't require a commute."

"The version of this that fails is the one where leadership calls it a compromise and both sides hear that as nobody winning," said Priya Ahluwalia, who led the internal working group that developed the policy. "Anchor days aren't a smaller version of five days in the office. They're a different tool, built for a different job."

Millbrook is publishing the hybrid-anchor framework, including its internal case for the model and its responses to the most common objections raised during the review, for any organization weighing the same choice. The company said it plans to publish a follow-up assessment after two full quarters of anchor-day operation.

**About Millbrook Software**
Millbrook Software builds workflow software for operations and program-management teams. The company is headquartered in Columbus, Ohio, with employees working from multiple states, including several markets where it has no physical office. Millbrook publishes select internal policies and frameworks publicly when it believes the reasoning behind them is useful beyond its own walls.
