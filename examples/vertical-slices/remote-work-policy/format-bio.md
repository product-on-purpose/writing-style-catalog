# About the Author

Priya Ahluwalia is the Policy Working Group Lead at Brackenridge Group, where she turned an undefined "flexible" work-location policy into a ratified position with a name and a shape: two mandatory anchor days, Tuesday and Thursday, for shared collaboration, and three fully flexible days for everything else. Reaching that position meant chartering a working group across HR, Facilities, Engineering, and Legal, then putting formal answers in writing to the two objections that mattered most: the office-first case for daily proximity, and the remote-only case for talent access and accommodations.

She has since published the reasoning behind that decision as hybrid-anchor, an open framework other companies can adopt directly instead of re-litigating the same office-or-remote binary from scratch. The same instinct ran through her earlier HR roles at Denholm Industries and Ashworth & Vale, where she built the manager-training and accommodation processes that hybrid teams still depend on: a contested policy question gets resolved by writing the position down, not by leaving it ambiguous. Priya is based in Columbus, Ohio, and continues to write and speak about workplace policy design for teams working through the same return-to-office argument Brackenridge already had.
