Dear Carolyn Marsh,

I am writing to apply for the Operations Coordinator opening on your team. Your posting is more candid than most: it says this role was held by one person for twenty-six years, and that continuous documentation is now a stated part of the job rather than an afterthought, which is exactly the kind of role I do well.

At Oakmere Utilities, I took over vendor escalation when the coordinator who had owned it for eleven years retired without a written map of who to call or why. I spent my first quarter rebuilding it from old call logs, invoice footers, and two former colleagues' memories, then made sure it never depended on one person's memory again: every vendor change, every renegotiated contract, every new contact got logged the same week it happened, not the year someone needed it and could not find it. When our first multi-vendor outage hit the following year, the map held. That habit is not a project I finished. It is how I have worked for the seven years since.

I do not bring twenty-six years of history with Crestfield. No outside candidate does, and I would not pretend otherwise in an interview. What I bring is a demonstrated record of doing exactly the kind of continuous documentation your team has just decided, formally, to make standard practice, and doing it without being asked, before the gap gets expensive.

I would welcome the opportunity to interview for this role. Please let me know a time that works this week or next.

Sincerely,
Teresa Aldana
