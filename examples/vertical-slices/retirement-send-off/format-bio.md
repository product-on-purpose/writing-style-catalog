# Howard Thayer
*Retirement spotlight - Crestfield Group Operations*

Howard Thayer spent twenty-six years as the Operations Coordinator at Crestfield Group, joining in June 2000 and holding the role, unchanged in title, through his last day on June 27, 2026. In his final months, he worked with Dana Reyes and Marcus Okonkwo across two sessions to write down what had never been written down: the vendor escalation paths, the four utility contacts that existed only in his personal contact list, and the sequence the team should follow when the automated alerts do not tell the full story. The result is the runbook the operations team now uses in his place.

Howard is remembered less for what he managed than for what he prevented. Priya Sandhu, Ben Holter, and four other colleagues he mentored over the years contributed to an internal archive of his method: how he framed a problem for someone who was panicking, how he absorbed a situation before he spoke. None of them describe being supervised by him. They describe him being there.

At the all-hands send-off on June 25, Howard spoke for four minutes. He said he was proud of the team and that he did not want to be the reason anyone felt stuck. It was, colleagues said afterward, exactly the kind of thing he would say.
