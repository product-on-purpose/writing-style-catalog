**Crestfield Group Formalizes Knowledge Continuity Practice After 26-Year Veteran Retires**

*New quarterly practice responds to the departure of Operations Coordinator Howard Thayer, whose institutional knowledge had never been fully written down*

HARTFORD, Conn., June 30, 2026 - Crestfield Group today announced a new quarterly knowledge continuity practice for its Operations department, adopted in direct response to the retirement of Howard Thayer, who served as Operations Coordinator for twenty-six years before his last day on June 27. Effective immediately, team leads across the department will document, on a recurring quarterly basis, the decisions, vendor relationships, and operational constraints that shape their areas of responsibility.

The policy responds to a gap the department found difficult to close in the two months before Thayer's departure. Thayer, who joined Crestfield Group in June 2000, carried vendor escalation paths and incident-response judgment that had never been requested in writing until it was needed. Working with colleagues Dana Reyes and Marcus Okonkwo across sessions in May and June, he helped produce an incident-response runbook covering vendor contacts, including four utility contacts that had existed only in his personal records, and the decision sequence the team should follow when automated alerts do not tell the full story. System access and vendor credentials transferred to three named successors by June 20.

Under the new practice, each team lead owns the content, format, and length of their own quarterly entry, and the cadence itself is not optional. Crestfield Group said the goal is to reduce the risk of institutional knowledge concentrating in a single person, a risk the department had suspected but confirmed only after Thayer's departure. The company has also designated two senior individual contributors to carry forward the informal mentoring role Thayer held for two decades, without creating a new formal title for it.

"We didn't lose a job description when Howard left. We lost twenty-six years of judgment that was never going to fit in a runbook," said Carolyn Marsh, Operations Lead, Crestfield Group. "The best we can do now is make sure the next person like Howard isn't carrying that much alone, and that we are not waiting until someone's last two months to start writing it down."

Colleagues marked Thayer's departure at an all-hands gathering on June 25, attended by 40 employees in person and 18 more remotely. Crestfield Group expects to complete its first test of the new runbook without Thayer's direct involvement during the third quarter, through an on-call rotation running to September 30. A review of the department's knowledge base against the incident types Thayer most frequently handled is scheduled for August 15.

**About Crestfield Group**
Crestfield Group operates the systems, vendor relationships, and incident response that keep its business units running day to day. Its Operations department is responsible for coordination across those functions and for the continuity of the knowledge that supports them.
