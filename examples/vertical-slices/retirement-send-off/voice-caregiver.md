Howard is going to leave, and that is the thing we need to say first.

Not "Howard is retiring" in the way that sounds like a ceremony. Howard is going to stop coming in. His desk will be empty on a Monday morning when something goes wrong - and something will go wrong - and the instinct to walk to the end of the hall will not be rewarded anymore. That is what this is.

He spent twenty-six years here. Most of it at the same desk, in the same role, and that was not inertia. That was a choice. He understood that the most useful thing you can do in a place is stay long enough to become the memory of it. He was the person you called when you could not remember why the old system worked the way it did, or when you needed to know who to trust in an unfamiliar part of the building, or when you just needed someone to sit with you while the crisis ran its course.

He mentored people without naming it that. He answered questions at the end of the day when it was inconvenient. He noticed when someone went quiet in a meeting and found them afterward. He never said "I'm proud of you" because he did not need to. You knew.

Several of you are in careers that exist because of what Howard did quietly, and without fanfare, and without asking for anything back.

It will not be easy to fill that. It probably will not be filled, exactly - it will be carried differently, by more people. That part is going to take some time.

Today we are just here to be with him for a moment. To say: we saw you, even when you were not looking. And we are grateful.
