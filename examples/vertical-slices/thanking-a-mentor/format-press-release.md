# Engineering Manager Nominates Former Mentor for Internal Honor

*Nomination traces to a decade-old decision to sponsor an unproven analyst for a migration nobody expected her to lead.*

PORTLAND, June 30, 2026 - Sable Marchetti, an engineering manager, has nominated her former manager, Dana Forsythe, for the company's internal mentorship award, citing a decision Forsythe made ten years ago to put an unproven analyst forward for a role she was not ready to hold. Forsythe is now Senior Director of Data Platforms. Marchetti recognized the full weight of that decision only this year, after using the same approach on a report of her own.

In March 2016, Forsythe put Marchetti forward to lead the Alderton platform migration, a six-month project with senior stakeholders watching, though Marchetti had no track record at that scope. Forsythe nominated her anyway, stayed close for the length of the project, answered the same questions more than once, and declined to take the work back the one time it was offered. The migration shipped, slightly late, and left Marchetti with a belief she could lead something she had not done before.

Marchetti says she did not recognize the cost of that patience until February, when she nominated Priya Osei to lead the Cassava data-pipeline rebuild over some internal skepticism. In the third week of March, with Osei stuck on the handoff logic, Marchetti considered taking the work back and did not; Osei's team is now four weeks ahead of schedule. Drafting Osei's review this spring, Marchetti recognized the pattern Forsythe had run on her a decade earlier.

"I only recognized my own pattern with Priya because I had watched Dana run it first, on a project nobody expected her to lead," said Sable Marchetti, engineering manager. "She spent six months absorbing my uncertainty and never made the call for me. I wanted that on the record, not in a thank-you note she could set aside."

"I didn't know the pattern I was benefiting from had a ten-year history until Sable told me," said Priya Osei, lead of the Cassava data-pipeline rebuild. "It changed how hard I was willing to try, knowing someone was deliberately choosing not to take over."

No one on the communications team asked for this. Marchetti chose the format built to travel from source to reader without being softened in the retelling, because this was not a story she wanted softened. The award will be decided later this year. Marchetti says the nomination stands regardless of outcome, and that she still plans to tell Osei that the patience Osei received this year did not originate with Marchetti at all.

**About Dana Forsythe**
Dana Forsythe is Senior Director of Data Platforms, leading the group that grew out of the migration she sponsored a decade ago. She has spent the ten years since applying the same approach to every report in her group: nominate before they feel ready, stay visibly available, let them make the call themselves.
