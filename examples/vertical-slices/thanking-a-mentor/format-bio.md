Dana Forsythe is Senior Director of Data Platforms, leading the group that grew out of a migration she sponsored a decade ago. In March 2016, with senior stakeholders watching and a safer option available, she put an unproven analyst forward to lead the Alderton platform migration rather than naming an established mid-level manager to the role. She stayed close for the length of the project, answering the same questions more than once, and did not take the work back the one time it was offered to her in a moment of panic.

That was not a one-time bet. Over the ten years since, Forsythe has kept to the same three moves for developing the people who report to her: nominate before they feel ready, stay visibly available, and let them make the call themselves. Sable Marchetti, the analyst from Alderton, has since carried the pattern to her own team. Earlier this year she nominated a colleague, Priya Osei, to lead the Cassava data-pipeline rebuild, then gave Osei the same room Forsythe had once given Marchetti.

Forsythe is this year's nominee for the company's internal mentorship award, put forward by Marchetti. Asked why, Marchetti said she only recognized her own pattern with Osei because she had watched Forsythe run it first, on a project nobody expected her to lead.
