"Ten years ago I was an analyst at Ashgrove with two smaller engagements behind me and no belief I was ready to lead the Alderton platform migration. Dana Forsythe put my name forward anyway. She pointed at the two smaller engagements, not my doubts, and moved the nomination forward. Then she stayed close instead of taking over: attended my first two stakeholder meetings saying almost nothing, corrected me privately after I got something wrong in the third, and refused to take the project back the one time I tried to hand it to her in a panic. It shipped. I ran the post-mortem alone. Last February I nominated a colleague the same way, and she is now four weeks ahead of schedule. I never thanked Dana properly for any of it. If Dana offers to put your name forward for something you don't feel ready for, say yes."

- Sable Marchetti, Director, Data Platform Engineering, Ashgrove Systems
