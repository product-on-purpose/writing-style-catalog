The expression on Marcos's face when I told him he would lead the platform migration stopped me for a moment. It was not quite surprise. It was more like a person searching for a handhold they were not sure would hold.

I had seen that expression before.

---

*"For this reason I remind you to fan into flame the gift of God, which is in you through the laying on of my hands. For God gave us a spirit not of fear but of power and love and self-control."*
(2 Timothy 1:6-7)

---

Paul writes this to Timothy from prison. He does not soften his circumstances or pretend the road ahead is easy. And still, what he reaches for is not instruction or a plan. He reaches for remembrance. Fan it into flame. The gift is already there. I put my hands on it. I saw it before you did.

What this passage does not say is just as important as what it says. It does not say "here is how you do this." It does not say "here is what to do when you get it wrong." It says: remember what was placed in you. Remember who named it first. That is the work of a particular kind of mentor - not to transfer a skill set but to witness what is already present and call it by its right name, before the person can name it themselves.

Ten years ago, Dana told me I would lead the Meridian account transition. It was a project that sat three years above my apparent experience level, and I told her as much. She said, "I know. Do it anyway." Then she did something that turned out to be harder than the assignment itself: she stayed close without hovering. When I stumbled on the first vendor call, she let me finish. She did not correct me in the room. Afterward, she asked only, "What would you do differently?" She asked some version of that question every week for six months.

When I gave Marcos that same kind of assignment last month, I heard Dana in my own voice. More than that - I felt what it must have cost her to hold back. Patience like that is not passive. It is an act of deliberate restraint, repeated week after week, trusting that what she had placed her hands on would hold.

---

Paul tells Timothy to fan what is already there. That is not the metaphor of a person starting a fire from nothing. It is the metaphor of someone who kneels close and tends a flame that already burns.

You may have someone in your life who tended yours before you could see the heat. Today might be the day to tell them.

---

*Lord, make me quick to name what I see in others, and slow to rescue them from the work of becoming it. Give me patience to stay close. Give me wisdom to stay out of the way. And remind me to thank the ones who did that for me.*
