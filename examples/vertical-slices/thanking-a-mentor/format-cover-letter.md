Dear Dana,

I'm writing to apply for the VP, Platform Engineering role you opened last week. Before I make the case, I want to name where my claim to it actually comes from: you tested this exact question once already, on the Meridian rebuild, ten years ago, and I have spent the decade since finding out whether the answer held.

The posting asks for two things above the rest: someone who can own outcomes at this scope without a prior track record at it, and someone who builds the next layer of leads instead of just delivering personally. On the first, my evidence is Meridian itself. You nominated me for full lead, not a co-lead arrangement, over an established mid-level candidate who was the safer call. I made the early scope and staffing calls myself with close, non-directive support, and reached full autonomous authority by month four. We shipped a few weeks behind the original target and handed the finished model to the teams it was built to serve. That project's decision record is still the reference the org points new leads to when scoping a rebuild this size. I did not set out to write the template; I just made the calls.

On the second, my evidence is this year. In February I nominated Priya Osei to lead the Cassava data-pipeline rebuild over real internal skepticism about her readiness. She stalled in week three of March on the handoff logic, wanted to give the work back, and I did not take it. She is four weeks ahead of her original schedule now. I have filled two of our four lead roles the same way since, and every time, I am running a method I did not invent.

Here is the direct ask: interview me for the role, on the same terms as anyone else, and hold my case to the same bar. Separately, and regardless of what you decide, I would like twenty minutes that is not about the job. I have owed you that conversation for ten years.

Sincerely,
Sable Marchetti

P.S. - It will look strange to get a cover letter from someone you already vouched for once without requiring an application. I am not fixing an oversight. I wanted the case for what you did to exist in writing, addressed to you, before I asked you for anything else.
