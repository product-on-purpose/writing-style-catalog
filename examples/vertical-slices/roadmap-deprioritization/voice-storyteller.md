In early June, Marcus pulled Priya into a conference room with a whiteboard still smeared from the previous meeting. He drew two timelines. One said "Billing Migration." The other said "Insights." He didn't say anything for a second, just looked at the board.

"They're touching," Priya said.

"They're more than touching," he said.

That's what we need to share with you. The billing migration that started in May - the one we flagged as an elevated-risk dependency - ran long. Not slightly long. The kind of long where the team was pulling late nights through July and the Insights feature cards sat untouched in the backlog, stacking up. We made a call: ship the migration correctly, or ship Insights half-built by the end of Q3. Half-built Insights would have been a dashboard with no drill-down, filters that didn't save, and export buttons that went nowhere. We've seen that version in demos. It isn't the thing we promised you.

Insights is now on the Q1 roadmap. That's a real date, not a holding slot.

Before Q1 - this week, actually - we're shipping a CSV export of the underlying data. It won't have the charts or the filter panes. But if your team has been waiting to run analysis on churn by segment, usage by plan tier, or seat activation rates, the data is there and you can pull it into whatever you already use. We'll send instructions with the release, and we've asked Deb on the customer success team to reach out individually to the accounts who were counting on Insights most.

One thing Marcus said in that conference room: "We could ship it in September and spend October explaining why it doesn't work, or we could ship it right in January." That felt true enough to hold the whole decision. We chose January.
