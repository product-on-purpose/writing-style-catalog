There is a particular kind of disappointment you carry when something promised is not delivered on time. You made plans around it. You told others about it. You arranged expectations - yours and theirs - in a particular order, and now that order needs to be rearranged. You know this feeling. It is not catastrophic, but it is real, and it deserves an honest word before anything else.

The writer of Lamentations 3 does not rush past grief: "He has driven me away and made me walk in darkness rather than light" - and only after sitting in that honestly does he arrive at "Yet this I call to mind and therefore I have hope." The order matters. You do not get to the hope by pretending the hard part was not hard.

So here is the hard part: Insights is not shipping in Q3. The billing-system migration ran long, consumed the engineering capacity we had set aside for the dashboard, and what we could deliver by the original date would not be what we promised you. We are not willing to ship half-built work and call it done.

This is disappointing, and it is also the right call. Both things are true, and we will not flatten either one to make this easier to read.

Here is what you do have this quarter: a CSV export of the underlying data, available now, so you can pull it into whatever tools you already use and run your own analysis. It is not the dashboard. It is access to the numbers, and for some of what you need, it may carry you through. Insights moves to Q1, and we will build it the way it deserves to be built.

You may have customers or conversations of your own to re-set. We want to help you carry those. Reach out to your account contact and let us know what you are walking into - we owe you the support to do this well, and we are not leaving you to handle it alone.
