Dear Hiring Team,

I am writing to apply for the Director of Product role at Basalt Analytics. I have led product for Meridian's usage-analytics surface since 2023, and this year that role required exactly the judgment an analytics vendor's roadmap depends on: holding a commitment accountable to what was promised, even when the easier path was to ship on the date and let the gaps surface later.

In Q3, a mandatory billing-system migration consumed the engineering capacity we had set aside to finish the Insights dashboard, which was already committed to enterprise accounts on a fixed date. What we had in front of us was missing the two capabilities those accounts had specifically been promised: saved-view persistence and scheduled-report delivery. I made the call to defer the dashboard to Q1 2027 rather than ship it incomplete, and had engineering shipping a CSV export of the same underlying data within two weeks so customers could keep working with their numbers while the full build caught up. Sales and customer success had the deferral and the stopgap plan in hand before any customer heard about the change secondhand, and I took the revised target to leadership myself and got sign-off before it reached a single customer-facing team.

That same quarter, I locked a six-view Q1 scope with engineering so the deferral bought a better dashboard, not just a later one. I also now run a cross-functional capacity review with engineering leadership every quarter, specifically so a conflict like the billing migration surfaces in planning instead of in a customer's inbox. That is the discipline I would bring to Basalt Analytics: fewer surprises reaching your customers, and a roadmap that tells the truth about what it can deliver and when.

I would welcome the chance to interview for this role and walk you through how the Insights deferral played out, start to finish.
