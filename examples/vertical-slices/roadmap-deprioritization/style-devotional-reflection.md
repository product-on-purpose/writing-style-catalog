The moment it became clear was not dramatic. Someone pulled up a spreadsheet of remaining engineering weeks, and the numbers simply did not close. There was no argument to be had. The quarter had made its own decision.

We had promised you Insights. We said it plainly, and we meant it. The analytics dashboard was real to us - not as a feature on a list, but as the thing that would finally let you see your data the way you have been asking to see it. We had planned it. Prototyped it. Talked about it with you, some of us, over calls where you described exactly what you needed. We carried that weight with us.

There is something particular about the moment when a commitment you made in good faith runs into something you did not foresee. You have probably been in a version of this yourself - not necessarily a software feature, but something you promised and believed you could keep, and then watched a circumstance arrive that the promise had not accounted for. The right action becomes clearer than it was a moment before. You can deliver something half-built, which satisfies neither the promise nor the person you made it to. Or you can say the thing you do not want to say: not this quarter.

The billing migration was not optional, and it consumed the engineering capacity that Insights needed. We made a choice to let that run through cleanly rather than stitch two incomplete things together. That choice came at a cost, and the cost was yours to bear as much as ours. We do not say that lightly.

What we can give you before September is a CSV export of the underlying data - not the dashboard we promised, but the data itself, accessible, ready for the spreadsheet or BI tool you already use. It is not what we said. It is what we can stand behind right now.

Insights is scheduled for Q1. We will bring it forward as soon as the ground is clear.

We are not asking you to make peace with this today. We are asking only: can you stay with us until January? The work has not been abandoned. It has been held.
