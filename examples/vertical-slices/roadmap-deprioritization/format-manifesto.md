# Late Beats Broken

*Issued the week we tell four customers that Insights will not ship this quarter.*

Every roadmap breaks a promise it made in good faith, sooner or later. The standard response, at most companies we've worked for before Meridian, is to manage the breakage quietly: soften the language, leave the old date technically alive on a slide somewhere, ship a smaller feature under the original name, and let the gap get explained in a footnote if it gets explained at all. We are rejecting that approach, in public, this week, with a real example instead of a hypothetical one.

Here is what we believe instead.

1. **A commitment belongs to the people it was made to, not to the team that made it.** When we cannot keep one, they hear it from us directly and first, in specific terms, not through a status page that quietly changes color or a release note three paragraphs deep. Four customers were given a date for Insights. Those four customers are being contacted directly this week, not left to find out secondhand.

2. **We do not ship the appearance of a feature to protect a date.** A dashboard without the saved views and scheduled reports our customers were specifically promised is not a smaller Insights. It is a different, worse product wearing the name. Offered that choice, we miss the date instead.

3. **A deferred commitment gets a quarter and a real date, not a "soon."** Insights moves to Q1 2027, with a target date of March 13. We are naming it in public because a claim that can later be checked is the only kind worth making now.

4. **Partial value now beats total silence until later.** Where we can put something real into someone's hands while the full thing gets built, we ship it, and we say plainly that it is a bridge, not the destination. The CSV export shipping before the end of this quarter is not a smaller Insights either. It is not Insights at all, and we are not letting anyone describe it that way.

5. **The team whose tradeoff caused the miss owns the conversation.** A mandatory, regulatory-driven billing migration, not a discretionary one, took the engineering capacity we had set aside for Insights. That tradeoff was ours to make. We do not hand the explaining to sales or customer success and step back from it.

6. **A directional date is labeled as one until it is closed.** We are naming Q1 2027 before the capacity behind it is fully locked, and we are saying so plainly instead of letting an early date sound more settled than it is.

What we stand against is the whole quiet playbook: the roadmap slide that never updates, the rescoped feature still marketed under its original promise, a delay that only shows up in a changelog nobody reads, and "more details soon" used to move our own discomfort about the date onto the person who is waiting on it. None of that is exactly dishonest. It is designed to protect the team that broke the promise instead of the people who were counting on it, and we are done building it that way.

What we stand for is the opposite of each of those, held at the same time: the falsifiable date over the comfortable one, the smaller true thing shipped now over the bigger vague thing promised later, and our own names on the tradeoff that caused the delay, not just on the plan for fixing it.

So, plainly: the CSV export ships before the end of this quarter, built by Dario Reyes's team, the same engineers who just finished the billing migration that displaced Insights in the first place. Jordan Park is contacting each of the four affected accounts directly this week. Insights is committed for Q1 2027, March 13 is the date we are asking to be held to, and we will put the next update in writing the moment anything about that changes. If you are the one delivering news like this, on any team, not just ours, take the standard for yourself: give the real date, name what people get in the meantime, and put your name on both. We would rather be caught missing a date we announced ourselves than be trusted for a promise we never meant to keep at full strength.

- Maya Chen, Product Lead, on behalf of the Insights team
