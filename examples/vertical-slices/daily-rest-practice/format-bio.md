# Renata Cole
*Contributor bio - Ashgrove Partners internal blog, "The Weekly Stop" series*

Renata Cole is a director of client services at Ashgrove Partners, where the working assumption for years was continuous availability: a productive week was one in which nothing sat unanswered. She has since committed to a different rule, one full day of rest for every seven, during which she does not produce work, complete tasks, or check messages or notifications. As she wrote in this week's installment, the day is not a lighter version of a workday; it is a structurally different kind of day.

The rule did not hold the first time. An earlier version of the practice ran six weeks before a deadline pulled it under, and Renata did not return to it for eleven months. The current run has now held for fourteen weeks, including the week she moved her phone into a drawer for a full ten hours, up from four the week before. She is now extending the boundary earlier, from Saturday evening to Friday sundown, and working to name, before July 5, the one channel she is most likely to rationalize checking. Renata is based in Minneapolis.
