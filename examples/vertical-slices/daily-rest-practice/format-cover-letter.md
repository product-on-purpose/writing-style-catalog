Dear Hiring Team,

I am applying for the Operations Lead role on your team. The strongest evidence I have for the role will not show up as a line on the resume: a fourteen-week discipline of holding one full day of rest per week, tracked with the same rigor I would bring to any system I owned professionally. It is the closest thing I have to direct proof that I can make a commitment hold up under real pressure, not just in a quiet week.

The record is not a success story dressed up for this letter. A first attempt at the same commitment ran six weeks in early 2025 before a deadline overrode it, and I did not return to it for eleven months. When I restarted in March, I wrote a short decision record naming the alternatives I had already tried and rejected, then tracked the outcome week by week instead of trusting my sense of how it was going. The practice is now three consecutive weeks into holding through real pressure, which is the point the first attempt never reached. That gap, between six weeks and three consecutive weeks under load, is the entire case: I do not just start disciplines, I instrument them, and I keep the ones that survive contact with a real deadline.

The habit that would transfer most directly to this role is naming a risk before it is comfortable to name. The most recent review of my own practice rated one goal Not Met: interrupting a habit of privately tallying whether each rest day was "worth it." No mitigation is in place yet, and the review said so directly instead of rounding a partial result up to a win because the recent weeks had gone well. That is the same standard I would bring to reporting status on your systems: the risk that is still open, not the one that is already solved.

I would welcome the chance to talk this through. Is there a time this week that works for a call?

Sincerely,
Daniel Weiss
