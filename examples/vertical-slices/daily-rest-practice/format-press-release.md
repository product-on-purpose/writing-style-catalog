**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**rest-day Marks Fourteenth Week Since Restart**

*Second attempt has already run more than twice as long as the first*

HOME, June 22, 2026 - rest-day, a personal practice of setting aside one full day each week for rest, has marked its fourteenth week since being recommitted to - more than double the six-week run the practice held before an earlier attempt collapsed under a work deadline. The current run held for a second consecutive week over the most recent seven-day period.

The most recent successful week ran from Saturday at 8 p.m., when the phone went into a drawer, through Sunday at 6 a.m. - a ten-hour window, up from four hours the previous week. One work message went unanswered from Sunday afternoon until Monday morning, with no reply drafted in the meantime, the first time that has happened since the practice restarted. The weekly productivity review, which scores what the week produced, did not run.

The rule behind rest-day has not changed since it was adopted: no work output, no task completion, no checking of messages or notifications on the protected day. The practice originated from an observed pattern: weeks worked through without a full stop produced measurably slower decisions and narrower patience for difficult problems, compared with weeks that included one. A background habit of tallying whether the rest was "worth it" has been identified as the largest risk to the practice and has not yet been resolved. A smaller, second risk involves returning to work too quickly once the protected window closes.

"Two consecutive weeks is not a large number, and the fourteen weeks behind it took an eleven-month gap to get to," said Self, Sole Practitioner, rest-day. "What the count is actually tracking is whether next Sunday is still on the calendar. So far, it always has been."

"The most noticeable change is not the day itself," said a colleague, who asked not to be identified. "It is that everything else got a little slower to hear back from. The explanation, when it comes, takes longer than most people expect."

The next test, scheduled for the coming week, extends the protected window to begin Friday at sundown and hold through Sunday evening. The result will be reported next week, whichever way it goes.

**About rest-day**
rest-day is a personal practice, not a company, product, or service. Its rule is one day in seven set aside from work, task completion, and notifications. It has no roadmap, no funding, and no plan to scale past one household.
