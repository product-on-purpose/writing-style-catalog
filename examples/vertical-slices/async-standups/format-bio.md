Jordan Ellis is an engineering manager at Fernbridge Software, where they lead an eleven-person engineering team spread across four time zones: US Pacific, US Eastern, the UK, and India. Over the past eighteen months, Jordan grew the team from six engineers to eleven and rebuilt how it communicates, replacing a daily synchronous standup at 9am Pacific (9:30pm IST) with an async, Slack-based update format and a single Thursday working session reserved for conversation that actually needs everyone live. The redesign is documented in the team's ADR and process README, making the reasoning and the rollout discoverable to new hires joining the distributed team.

Before Fernbridge, Jordan spent nearly five years as a senior software engineer at Warrenton Analytics, where they led the migration of the company's primary billing service off a monolithic deployment. Jordan writes and speaks occasionally about distributed team management and async-first process design, drawing on the standup redesign at Fernbridge and the results from its first 30-day trial. Jordan is based in Seattle.
