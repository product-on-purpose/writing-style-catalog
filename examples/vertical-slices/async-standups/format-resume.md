## Experience

Engineering Manager | Fernbridge Software | March 2021 - Present
- Hired and onboarded 5 engineers over 18 months, growing a distributed engineering team from 6 to 11 across four time zones: US Pacific, US Eastern, UK, and India.
- Redesigned the team's daily status process, replacing a synchronous 9am Pacific standup that produced roughly 4 minutes of substantive content out of every 14 minutes and left India-based engineers attending an average of 3.2 of 5 weekly sessions versus 4.6 for US-based engineers.
- Shipped a three-field async template (Shipped, In progress, Blocked or at risk) posted to #team-standup by 10am local time, with a single 60-minute weekly working session on Thursdays reserved for discussion that needed real-time exchange.
- Raised on-time daily posting from 78% to 85.5% week over week during the first two weeks of a 30-day trial, tracked across 55 expected posts per week from an 11-engineer team.
- Cut blocker response time to a median of 18 minutes and a 90th percentile of 2 hours 40 minutes, replacing a process where blockers raised at 9am often went unaddressed until the afternoon.
- Brought India-based engineers to full weekday participation for the first time in the team's history and recovered approximately 70 minutes per engineer per week previously spent in synchronous status reporting.
- Documented the decision and rollout in an ADR, a process README, and a 30-day trial retro plan, making the rationale and template discoverable to new hires joining the distributed team.
