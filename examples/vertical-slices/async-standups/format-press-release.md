# Fernbridge Software Retires Daily Standup for Global Engineering Team

*A 30-day pilot cut blocker response time to 18 minutes and ended a nightly 9:30pm call for engineers in India*

SEATTLE, June 23, 2026 - Fernbridge Software has made asynchronous, written standups the permanent replacement for its daily 9am Pacific engineering meeting, following a 30-day pilot across its eleven-person engineering team spanning four time zones. The meeting had fallen at 9:30pm for engineers in India and produced roughly four minutes of decision-relevant content inside every fourteen-minute call. Engineers now post a structured written update to a shared channel by 10am their own local time instead of joining a live call.

Results held for the full pilot. On-time posting climbed from 78 percent in the first week to 85.5 percent in the second and stayed near that level through day 30. The median time between a flagged blocker and a substantive reply fell to 18 minutes, replacing a pattern where morning blockers often went unanswered until afternoon. Engineers based in India, whose attendance under the old system had averaged 3.2 of 5 sessions a week against 4.6 for US-based engineers, posted an update every weekday of the pilot for the first time in the team's history.

Under the new format, engineers write three fields - what shipped, what is in progress, and what is blocked or at risk - and tag whoever can resolve a blocker directly. An on-call engineer checks the shared channel each morning and responds to flagged blockers within 30 minutes during business hours. The daily meeting is replaced by a single 60-minute working session held weekly and reserved for discussion that genuinely needs everyone live; two concerns raised mid-pilot, including on-call triage load, were resolved by tightening the update format rather than by changing the rotation.

"We were not trying to make a statement about remote work. We were trying to stop asking a third of the team to sit through a status meeting after 9pm," said Jordan Ellis, Engineering Manager, Fernbridge Software. "The async format gives us back the information the meeting was supposed to produce, without the time zone tax on the people least able to push back on it."

Fernbridge Software says the format is now standard practice for the engineering organization, is included in onboarding for every new engineering hire, and has no further trial review scheduled. The company will revisit the format only if a future retrospective surfaces a problem it cannot handle.

**About Fernbridge Software**
Fernbridge Software's engineering organization is distributed across four time zones, with engineers based in the United States, the United Kingdom, and India. The company designs its internal processes for a distributed team by default and documents changes so new hires can run them without extra onboarding. Fernbridge Software is hiring engineers in all four of its current time zones.
