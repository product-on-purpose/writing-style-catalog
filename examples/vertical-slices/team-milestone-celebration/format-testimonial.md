*Featured on Northlane Platform Engineering's internal tools page.*

"Checkout touches every order we process, so a bad rollout was not an option we could risk. We built the new checkout as a separate service and used Waymark to move traffic over by cohort, starting at one percent. That gave us a way to be wrong safely. Waymark's rollback path pulled two cohorts back to the old flow, in October and December, without a customer noticing. We also held the full launch twice more while engineers ran down two bugs before either one reached a customer. Fourteen months is a long time to run two checkout systems side by side, but Waymark is the reason none of that time cost us an incident. Full cutover landed June 13 and held through peak traffic that weekend. I would not attempt a migration this size without it again."

- Priya Vasquez, Program Lead for Checkout Engineering, Northlane
