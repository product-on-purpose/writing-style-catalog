Dear Hiring Team,

I'm writing to apply for the Director of Engineering Programs role. Your posting asks for someone who has taken a live, revenue-critical system through a multi-quarter migration without customers noticing the transition, and that is the project I have just finished running.

For the past fourteen months I led Project Halyard, the rebuild of our checkout platform, as Program Lead at Thornbury Retail. We ran the new system alongside the five-year-old original, migrated traffic cohort by cohort starting at one percent, and reached full cutover in June with no customer-facing incident. That included exercising the rollback path twice during the rollout and holding two separate launch dates when engineers found defects the automated test suite had missed, rather than shipping around them. The rebuilt checkout has since held through its first full peak-traffic weekend, and it is already closing out the multi-year cart-abandonment problem it was funded to fix.

The harder part of those fourteen months was organizational, not technical: keeping a program funded and a team protected from schedule pressure when there was no visible feature to point to for months at a time. I owned the cross-functional coordination and the executive reporting that made two schedule slips a deliberate, defensible choice instead of a crisis, and I closed the program out myself with the final report to the wider organization. I've since written up the dual-track approach we used, specifically so the next team facing a migration this size does not have to start from zero, which is the kind of judgment I understand this role to be asking me to scale beyond a single program.

I would welcome the chance to make that case in person - I'm asking for an interview.
