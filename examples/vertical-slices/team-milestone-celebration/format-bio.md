Priya Vasquez is a Program Lead on the checkout engineering team. She spent the past fourteen months running Project Halyard, the ground-up rebuild of the company's checkout pipeline, and closed it out herself with the final status report to the wider organization.

The rebuild ran two live checkout systems side by side for the full fourteen months, migrating traffic cohort by cohort from a one percent canary up to a full cutover, while the legacy flow kept processing real orders the entire time. No customer ever saw a degraded checkout during the changeover. Two near-misses surfaced during the parallel run, both caught and resolved before a customer encountered them, and both times the team chose to slip the launch date rather than ship around an unresolved risk - a discipline Priya protected by keeping schedule pressure off the engineers who had to make that call.

The project closed in June 2026: the rebuilt checkout held through its first peak-traffic weekend without a rollback, and the legacy system moved into a scheduled decommission. Priya is now documenting the dual-track migration approach the team used, so the next hard rebuild does not have to start from zero.
