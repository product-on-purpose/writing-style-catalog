Subject: Your reliability-track CFP note on migration cost

Your CFP note for the reliability track said most migration talks stop at the uptime graph and skip what the migration actually cost to pull off. That line is the reason I'm writing.

We just closed a fourteen-month parallel-run rebuild of our checkout system: two near-misses that would have shipped broken if two different engineers hadn't caught them first, two slipped launch dates, and a cutover that held through the first peak weekend without a rollback. The slips are the actual talk, not the uptime number.

Would a fifteen-minute call be worth it, or should I just send the two-paragraph abstract instead?

Yuki Tanaka, PM, Project Halyard
