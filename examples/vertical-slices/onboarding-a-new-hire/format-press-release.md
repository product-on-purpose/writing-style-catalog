# Northlane Systems Adds Priya Rao to Backend Engineering Team

*New engineer completes two-week onboarding protocol with her first production deploy*

AUSTIN, Texas, July 3, 2026 - Northlane Systems today announced that Priya Rao joined the company's Backend Services team as a Software Engineer on June 22 and capped a structured two-week onboarding program with her first production deploy this week. Rao joins Northlane after nearly four years at Larkspur Systems, where she worked on backend billing and invoicing systems. Her ramp-up is the latest to run under the company's guided-pairing onboarding protocol, which pairs every new engineer with a named buddy and a project scoped before their first day.

Under the protocol, Rao's first two days were dedicated to access and tooling, tracked against a checklist owned by her named buddy, engineer Arjun Nair. Week one paired her with two guided walkthroughs, one on service topology and one on deployment and on-call tooling, alongside one-on-ones with the engineers who own the systems she now works in. In week two, Rao drove a pre-scoped change to a single service from design through code review to her own production deploy, with Nair pairing on any blockers along the way.

Backend Services ships to production daily and runs a shared on-call rotation across its engineers. New hires are excluded from that rotation for their first 30 days, time the team uses to build familiarity before adding pager load. Northlane adopted the protocol to close a gap it saw repeatedly: engineers who spent early weeks solving access problems alone, then shipped a first change too late for it to shape how the team saw them.

"We do not think onboarding should be a matter of luck," said Mei Chen, Onboarding Program Lead, Backend Services, Northlane Systems. "Priya had a named buddy, a scoped project, and an owner for every open question from her first morning. Two weeks later, her name was in the deployment log. That is the outcome we build toward with every engineer who joins this team."

"Having a project scoped before I even started meant I was never guessing what to work on," said Priya Rao, Software Engineer, Northlane Systems. "I traced a real production request in my first week and shipped a real fix by the start of my third."

Rao's two-week onboarding closes with a formal retrospective this week; the pairing relationship with her buddy typically continues informally afterward. Northlane Systems is hiring across its backend engineering organization and expects to run its next new hire through the same protocol.

**About Northlane Systems**
Northlane Systems builds backend infrastructure software for engineering teams that ship to production daily. Its Backend Services team pairs every new engineer with a named buddy and a pre-scoped first project during a structured two-week onboarding period. The company is based in Austin, Texas, with a distributed engineering organization.
