The first day is harder than it looks from the outside. Priya will arrive to a team chat she cannot yet post to, a codebase she has never navigated under pressure, and a room full of people who already know each other's shorthand. Start from there.

Week one is not about velocity. It is about belonging enough to ask a question.

Before she arrives, get the accesses done - all of them. The ticket tracker, the deployment tools, the on-call runbooks, the code repository. One gap in access on day one will cost her a piece of confidence each time she hits it. If something is still missing Monday morning, walk her through it together. Do not leave her to find the walls herself.

Day one, sit with her. Not a tour, not a wiki link. Open the codebase together and show her how you actually find your way in it - not how it is supposed to work. Those are different, and she cannot yet know which she is getting.

Name the on-call rotation early. Tell her she will not be primary for the first month. Tell her that shadowing one real incident with you is worth more than any runbook. She does not have to hold that fear by herself.

For the first change: look at the open work together and let her say which piece feels approachable. Then stay close - available for the small question she might not think is worth raising.

By the end of week two, she should have shipped something real. It does not have to be large. It has to be hers. A pull request that gets reviewed and merged by a real teammate is what turns "I work here" into "I belong here."

On the human side: use her name in standups. Acknowledge what she figured out. Tell her something true about the team - including what is genuinely hard about working here. She will trust you more for it.

This takes attention. That is all we are asking.
