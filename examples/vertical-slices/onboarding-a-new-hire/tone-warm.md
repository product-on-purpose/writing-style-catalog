Priya, I am glad you are here.

The first two weeks on a team like this can feel like a lot is happening at once - new faces, new systems, a codebase that rewards patience, and an on-call rotation that runs whether or not you feel ready for it. You do not need to know everything yet. That is not the goal.

Here is what matters in week one: getting you access, getting you oriented, and starting to understand the shape of the system. We will walk through your local setup together on day one and sort out any accounts that are still pending. Some of this is tedious - there is always a permission that takes an extra day to come through - and that is fine. Use the wait to read. The architecture overview is a good place to start; this team ships every day, and understanding what you are shipping into makes everything else click faster.

In week two, we will pair on a small change together. Not a toy exercise - a real one that ships. The goal is that you walk out of your second Friday having put something into production. That matters to me. It matters because it is yours, and because crossing that line changes how you feel about belonging to the team.

Who owns what is worth learning early. There is a map; we will go through it. When something breaks or confuses you, knowing who to ask is half the solution.

By the end of week two, you should feel like you work here - not just function here.
