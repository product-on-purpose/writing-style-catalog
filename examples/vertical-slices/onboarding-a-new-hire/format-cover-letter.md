I'm writing to apply for the Software Engineer, Backend Services role on your team. Nearly four years of owning production backend systems inside a live on-call rotation is exactly the background your posting's emphasis on daily deploys and shared on-call responsibility is asking for, and it is the kind of team I want to keep working on.

At Larkspur Systems, I redesigned the alert routing rules across two monitoring tools after the same production incident triggered three separate pages under three different alert names, and no single page told the full story. The fix collapsed those into one alert with a clear owner, shortened the time between a page firing and someone acknowledging it, and cut the number of after-hours pages the team absorbed. That is the work I want to keep doing here: not just responding to an incident, but changing the system around it so less of the load lands on one person at 2 a.m.

My time at Larkspur is in billing and invoicing, not the order and checkout paths this role covers, and I would rather name that gap than let it surface later. The domain is different, but the failure shape is not: a retry gap in a checkout flow and a duplicate alert in a billing pipeline are the same category of problem, and getting fast at recognizing that category is what nearly four years on call actually teaches you.

I would welcome the opportunity to talk through how that experience applies to your team's on-call load in an interview.
