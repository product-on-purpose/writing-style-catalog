The feedback is everywhere. Someone filed a ticket. Someone else said something in the chat channel. A customer mentioned it during a call, and you wrote it down somewhere. By Friday, you cannot remember which problems were mentioned twice and which ones were just mentioned once, loudly.

You probably have a spreadsheet. Or you had one. It was working until it was not.

That is the problem Tidemark is built for.

Tidemark is a small tool. It collects the feedback your team is already capturing - in the ticket tracker, in the chat channel, in the notes from customer calls - and brings everything into one place. Then it asks a simple question: how many people named this problem? It does the counting. It surfaces a ranked list. You can adjust the weights, add context, and share the result with anyone on your team.

No more Friday archaeology. No more deciding by whoever spoke last.

Tidemark opens for general access next week. You do not have to import your whole history on day one. Start with the last four weeks. See if the list surprises you.

If you are already a customer and have been waiting for this: thank you for waiting. The roadmap in Tidemark now shows what you asked for, and we will keep updating it as you use the tool.

If you are new here: welcome. You can sign up at the link below. There is a short walkthrough, and you do not have to finish it in one sitting.

The problem is not going to disappear. But you can start making it smaller, one week's feedback at a time.
