There is a kind of work that happens quietly, at the margins of most product teams' weeks - the gathering of what customers say, in the chat tool, in support tickets, in surveys that are only half-finished, in conversations that happened months ago and were never written down. This work is not glamorous. It is essential.

Tidemark is a tool built to honor that work.

It is, in plain description, a way for small teams to bring their scattered customer feedback into a single, ranked, shareable roadmap - one place where the signal inside all that noise can be seen clearly, held carefully, and acted on together. The feedback that lives in a dozen different places comes in. The roadmap that reflects what customers have actually said comes out.

What Tidemark does differently is refuse to let customer voices be flattened. A ranked roadmap produced by Tidemark is not an algorithm's general impression. It is a considered rendering of what your customers, taken together, have said matters - specific, traceable, shareable with anyone on the team who needs to understand why something is at the top of the list.

Tidemark launches next week. Teams who would like early access may request an invitation at tidemark.io. Those who have been carrying these voices in scattered places, and have wanted something better for them, will find that it is ready to receive them.
