Dear Hiring Team,

I'm writing to apply for the VP of Product role at Northlake Software. Since 2024, I've been Head of Product at Tidemark, where I took a feedback-to-roadmap tool from private beta through a public launch that shipped on the date and the positioning we set months earlier - the end-to-end ownership your posting names as the role's top requirement.

Your posting calls out launch ownership, from positioning through go-to-market, as the core of the job. That is the specific problem I solved at Tidemark. Feedback-synthesis tools default to a category label; I set our launch to lead with the problem instead - scattered feedback with no shared ranked view - after weighing three framing options with the go-to-market team. Twenty-two teams ran the full workflow in early access ahead of general availability, and every one completed it without filing a support ticket, the proof point the launch was built on. The positioning held, unchanged, across the landing page, the press brief, and cohort outreach through the June 30 launch, and press contacts used the same language back to us in their own notes.

The posting also asks for product leadership that scales past a small team. Tidemark's customers are two-to-twenty person teams, so I want to address that directly: before Tidemark, I owned feedback intake and prioritization at Bramwell Analytics, a mid-market analytics platform, where I consolidated four disconnected channels into one monthly review and built the scoring rubric that replaced ad hoc prioritization fights between sales and engineering. The pattern holds at either scale: make the defensible call before the data is clean, then build the structure that lets the team keep making it without me in the room.

I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how that experience applies to Northlake's roadmap.

Sincerely,
Marisol Veen
