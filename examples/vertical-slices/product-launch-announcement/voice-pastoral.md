There is a moment most small teams know. The feedback has been coming in - through the chat tool, tucked into support replies, scribbled in the notes from last week's call. You have heard your customers. The trouble is you cannot hold all of it at once, and when someone asks what comes next, you find yourself gesturing at a list that only half-exists, pieced together from three different places.

You know this feeling. It is not a failure of care. It is what happens when care outruns its container.

The writer of Proverbs names something like it: "Where there is no guidance, a people falls, but in an abundance of counselors there is safety." The movement that proverb tracks is not about quantity. It is about the difference between voices scattered and voices gathered. Wisdom that has no home cannot do its work. You have wisdom - your customers have given it to you - and right now it has nowhere to live.

That is the problem Tidemark was built to solve.

Tidemark takes the feedback scattered across your inbox, your support queue, the chat tool, and the ticket tracker, and draws it into a single ranked roadmap you can share, discuss, and actually build from. It launches next week.

We will be honest with you: Tidemark does not make the hard decisions for you. Deciding what to build next is still your work, and that work is genuinely difficult. What changes is that you no longer have to make those decisions from memory, or from whoever spoke most recently. You make them from the full picture - gathered, weighed, and visible to everyone on your team.

That is a different kind of meeting. That is a different kind of team.
