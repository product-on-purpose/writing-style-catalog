# The Tidemark Manifesto

*Why we built a roadmap tool, and what we refuse to build next.*

Every roadmap meeting we have ever sat in got decided the same way before anyone opened a scoring spreadsheet: whoever spoke last, loudest, or most recently won the room. Nobody in that room was lying. Nobody was working from the same set of facts either, and a prioritization framework cannot fix that on its own. We do not accept that this is simply how roadmaps get made. Tidemark opens to the public today, June 30, 2026, and we are saying so out loud.

Here is what we believe, on launch day and after it:

- **A roadmap is only as honest as the evidence underneath it.** Weigh requests by revenue, by frequency, by strategic fit, whatever your team has agreed matters, but do that work on one shared set of facts first. A framework cannot rescue evidence that was already in dispute. It only dresses the dispute up as math.
- **Small teams deserve the same rigor as teams with a dedicated research function, without needing to build one.** A team of three people who own a roadmap should not have to hire a researcher, sign an enterprise contract, or wait a quarter to know what their customers have actually been asking for.
- **A roadmap only one person can see is not a roadmap. It is a private opinion with a deadline.** Ranking the work is half the job. The other half is a link any stakeholder can open and trust without requesting an account first.
- **The workflow comes before the feature list.** Gather, rank, share - each has to work cleanly on its own before we add anything else to it. A long list of integrations does not fix a planning meeting that opens by re-litigating whose feedback is real.
- **Understanding a product should never cost a sales call.** A prospective user should be able to see exactly what a tool does before anyone asks for their calendar, their card, or their patience.

We stand against the private spreadsheet every team keeps and nobody names, the one whose sole purpose is settling arguments about what customers actually said. We stand against planning meetings that spend their first twenty minutes reconciling whose list is accurate before any real decision gets made. We stand against software priced and built for organizations with dedicated product operations teams, then sold down to teams of three who will pay for infrastructure they will never fully use. We stand against demos gated behind a pitch, where seeing the product requires first surviving the sales call meant to sell it to you.

We stand for one connected, ranked, shareable view, built from every source your team already uses: the support tickets, the chat threads, the ticket trackers, the survey exports, the notes from your last customer call. We stand for a free plan open to any individual, no time limit and no card required to start. We stand for a 20-minute walkthrough anyone can book directly, with no sales call attached to it. We stand for pricing anyone can read on the page: free to start, $29 per month for up to 15 seats, and a custom plan reserved for organizations that actually need SSO and audit logs, not offered as an upsell to teams that don't. We stand for shipping the core loop (gather, rank, share) before a single integration gets added to it.

Tidemark is open to any team starting today at tidemark.io. If your last planning meeting spent its opening stretch establishing whose feedback was real before anyone decided anything, we built this for you, and we are asking you to try it. Start free. Book the walkthrough if you want to see the product before you commit to anything. Bring the spreadsheet you have been keeping to settle arguments about what your customers said. We intend to make it unnecessary. Hold us to that. Do not just take our word for it.
