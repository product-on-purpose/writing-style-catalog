## Sermon

A sermon is a spoken address delivered to a gathered congregation that interprets a sacred text
and applies it to the lives of the listeners, moving from exposition to exhortation. It is
preached aloud, structured for the ear, and aimed at a community at worship. The preacher
stands before many - addressing them as a plural body sharing a common moment - and calls
them toward a response together.

The sermon earns its shape from the spoken word. Repetition, refrains, cadenced phrases that
build toward a climax, and the rhythm of the preacher's voice are not ornaments but structural
load-bearing elements. What reads as redundant on paper often lands as clarity when heard aloud.
A good sermon is not a written essay performed; it is a spoken event that may later be
transcribed.

### Canonical template

```
[Opening: hook, image, or question that establishes urgency or wonder]

[Scripture reading: the anchor text, read aloud in full]

[Exposition: 2-3 major moves, each interpreting a portion of the text]
  Move 1: What the text says - close reading of the passage
  Move 2: What the text means - theological or narrative unpacking
  Move 3 (optional): How the text pushes against common assumption or objection

[Illustration: a story, image, or analogy that makes the exposition concrete]

[Exhortation: the turn toward the congregation - what are they being called to do, believe, or become?]

[Closing invitation: a direct, simple call - often tied back to the opening image]
```

### When to use

Sunday worship services or gathered congregational settings, preaching to a live audience from a sacred text, calls to communal response aimed at the whole congregation, chapel or retreat sessions with a homiletical shape, written transcripts that must retain the oral proclamatory register.

### When not to use

Personal devotional reading designed for an individual working through scripture privately, academic biblical commentary or theological argument without a congregational audience, business or organizational communication even when motivational in tone.

### Pairs well with

`pastoral`, `storyteller`, `reverent`, `encouraging`, `classical-argument`

### Often confused with

**devotional-entry**: A devotional entry is a short-form spiritual piece designed for personal or small-group use, typically consumed in 5 to 10 minutes, that anchors to a scripture passage and moves from that text toward personal application for a single reader. A sermon is public, longer, and oral - one preacher addressing many listeners in a shared moment and calling them toward a response together, rather than prompting a single reader's quiet personal reflection.
