## Cold Outreach

A cold outreach message is a first contact with someone who has no prior relationship with the
sender. It cannot assume goodwill, recognition, or any shared context. Every line has one job:
to justify the next line. The format exists to earn a single reply from someone who has no
reason yet to give one.

The mechanism is recipient-first relevance. Effective cold outreach opens not with the sender's
name and title, but with a specific, researched observation about the recipient - something that
makes clear this message was not sent to anyone like them but to them specifically. That
specificity is what separates cold outreach from spam. The opening signals: I know something
about your work, and what I am about to say is worth your thirty seconds.

### Canonical template

```
Subject: [Short, specific, no "Introduction" or "Following up"]

[Opening: one specific observation about the recipient - something they wrote, built, or said
that is genuinely relevant to why you are reaching out. No self-introduction here.]

[Bridge: one to two sentences naming why you or your work is relevant to THAT observation.
The connection must be real, not aspirational.]

[Ask: one sentence, one request, low commitment. A reply, a 15-minute call, a yes/no question.]

[Sign-off: name only, or name plus one-line identifier. No company boilerplate.]
```

### When to use

Reaching out to a potential partner, collaborator, or customer you have never met; initiating a
professional relationship where no warm introduction exists; requesting a brief conversation or a
single piece of information from an expert; pitching yourself or your work to someone in a
position to act on it.

### When not to use

Writing to someone you already have a relationship with (use a direct email instead); situations
requiring formal correspondence or institutional letterhead; contexts where the recipient expects
a full introduction or a detailed proposal before engaging.

### Pairs well with

`direct-communicator`, `senior-consultant`, `diplomatic`, `confident`, `problem-solution`

### Often confused with

**email**: Email is a business message designed for the inbox scan - it operates within an
established professional context, leads with an explicit action request, and must provide self-contained context because the reader should be able to act without re-reading or asking a follow-up question. Cold outreach shares the
email channel but differs in every structural decision that matters: it addresses a complete
stranger, cannot assume goodwill or recognition, opens with the recipient's interest rather than
the sender's request, and has a single purpose - earning a first reply from someone with no
prior reason to give one.
