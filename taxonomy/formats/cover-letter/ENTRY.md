## Cover Letter

A cover letter accompanies a resume or application and makes the case for one specific person for
one specific role. It connects the applicant's experience to the posting's stated needs, in the
applicant's own voice, and asks for the interview. Unlike cold outreach, which contacts a stranger
with no posting and no established opening, a cover letter responds to an advertised opportunity
and assumes the reader is already evaluating candidates against a known set of needs.

The format is not a prose version of the resume. The resume lists; the cover letter argues. The
argument is always the same: given what you need and what I have done, an interview is the logical
next step. Everything else - the opener, the body, the close - exists to build that argument and
make it feel earned rather than asserted.

### Canonical template

```
[Header: Applicant name, contact info, date]
[Employer name, title, company, address - optional for email submissions]

Dear [Hiring Manager Name, or Hiring Team if name unknown],

[Opening: name the specific role and establish the applicant's direct claim to it. One to two sentences.]

[Body 1: connect the applicant's single strongest relevant experience to the posting's primary stated need.
Name the experience specifically - a result, a project, a problem solved.]

[Body 2 (optional): address a second stated need or preempt an obvious gap. Two to three sentences.]

[Closing: ask for the interview directly. One sentence. Do not hedge.]

Sincerely,
[Name]
```

### When to use

Applying for an advertised job posting that explicitly requests a cover letter; applying for a role
where the resume alone cannot convey the relevant fit; situations where a career change, gap, or
unconventional background needs framing; applications to organizations where cultural fit and voice
matter alongside the credential; any application where the job description names specific values or
priorities the applicant can address directly.

### When not to use

Applications that explicitly state no cover letter or use form-only intake; initiating contact with
a company that has no open posting (use cold outreach instead); submitting to job boards where the
system strips attachments and only the resume reaches the screener.

### Pairs well with

`senior-consultant`, `direct-communicator`, `confident`, `diplomatic`, `problem-solution`

### Often confused with

**cold-outreach**: Cold outreach is a first-contact message to a stranger with no prior relationship
and no established opening - it earns a reply by leading with a specific, researched observation
about the recipient, because there is no shared context to draw on yet. A cover letter differs in
every structural decision that matters: it responds to an advertised opportunity, addresses a reader
who is already evaluating candidates against known needs, and leads with the applicant's direct
claim to the role rather than an observation about the employer.
