## Ad Copy

Ad copy is short promotional text written to grab attention and drive a single action - usually
under tight character limits. A headline, a line or two of benefit, and a call to action make
up its entire structure. Every word fights for the click. The defining constraint is compression:
where a landing page earns conversion through depth, ad copy earns the right to be clicked in a
few characters or a few seconds. Its job is not to close the deal - it is to make the deal
irresistible enough to pursue.

Unlike a landing page, which is the full conversion experience a click leads to - value
proposition, benefits organized for scanning, objections handled, social proof, and CTA across a
whole page - ad copy is the brief hook that earns the click in the first place. The landing page
justifies the promise; ad copy makes the promise urgent and specific enough to act on first.

### Canonical template

```
[Headline: hook - outcome, problem, or curiosity gap - 5-15 words]
[Body/reinforcing line (optional): one concrete benefit or mechanism - 10-20 words]
[CTA: action verb + object - "Start free trial", "Get the guide", "See how it works" - 2-5 words]
```

### When to use

Writing paid search ads, social ads, or display placements, crafting email subject lines intended to lift open rate, promoting a product or offer in a constrained format, supporting a campaign where the ad drives traffic to a landing page, testing multiple value angles quickly before committing to a full campaign.

### When not to use

Explaining a complex product that requires context before evaluation, building credibility with an audience unfamiliar with the brand, internal communications or proposals where promotional register would undermine the message.

### Pairs well with

`product-thinker`, `direct-communicator`, `confident`, `playful`, `problem-solution`

### Often confused with

**landing-page**: A landing page is web copy written to convert a visitor into a single action - the full conversion experience the click leads to. It leads with a clear value proposition, organizes benefits for scanning, handles the obvious objections before the visitor raises them, adds social proof, and drives toward one CTA across a complete page. Ad copy is the brief hook that earns the click to arrive at that page. The landing page is what a reader finds after acting on the ad; ad copy is what makes them act in the first place.
