## Styles

| ID | Name | One-liner | Status |
|----|------|-----------|--------|
| `chronological-narrative` | Chronological Narrative | Time order is the primary organizing principle - first this, then that, then what came after - with no thematic restructuring. | stable |
| `classical-argument` | Classical Argument | The Toulmin structure of claim, grounds, warrant, and rebuttal - making a position defensible through explicit reasoning. | stable |
| `comparison-contrast` | Comparison-Contrast | Places two or more options, ideas, or states side by side to illuminate the differences that matter. | stable |
| `decision-log` | Decision Log | A real-time record of context, options considered, criteria used, and reasoning - capturing how a decision was reached, not justifying it after the fact. | stable |
| `definitional` | Definitional | Leads with a definition and elaborates through its facets, edge cases, and what-it-is-not - the definition is the load-bearing first move. | stable |
| `devotional-reflection` | Devotional Reflection | A slow, inward-turning movement that takes a scriptural or spiritual observation and follows it into personal and communal life. | stable |
| `dialectic` | Dialectic | Thesis, antithesis, synthesis - states the position, states the strongest opposing position, and moves to a synthesis that takes both seriously. | stable |
| `diataxis-explanation` | Diataxis Explanation | The Diataxis "Explanation" mode - oriented toward understanding rather than action, building a conceptual model for the reader. | stable |
| `executive-summary` | Executive Summary | Inverted-pyramid writing for decision-makers - recommendation first, supporting analysis second, structured so that stopping after paragraph one still leaves the reader equipped to act. | stable |
| `frequently-asked-questions` | Frequently Asked Questions | Sections are real reader questions in their natural phrasing, ordered by likelihood of being asked rather than by logical flow. | stable |
| `how-to-tutorial` | How-To Tutorial | Task-first writing that takes a reader from "I don't know how" to "I did it" by organizing every decision around user goals, not system features. | stable |
| `layered-disclosure` | Layered Disclosure | Progressively reveals depth - the first paragraph serves the casual reader completely, and each subsequent section adds detail for those who want it. | stable |
| `narrative-case-study` | Narrative Case Study | A story with a before, a turning point, and an after - using one specific real situation to make a general principle concrete and trustworthy. | stable |
| `problem-solution` | Problem-Solution | Frames the piece as a diagnosis followed by a remedy - establishes the pain before the cure. | stable |
| `socratic-inquiry` | Socratic Inquiry | Advances by asking questions the reader answers in their own head - the conclusions are inferences, not assertions. | stable |
