# 0007 - LLM-Generated Examples Require Human Review Before Promotion to Stable

## Status

Accepted

## Context

Phase 0 and Phase 1 will lean on LLM-generated examples to populate vertical slices. The scale of content needed makes human-only authoring impractical on a reasonable timeline. Phase 0 requires 20 examples (5 per axis, 1 per entry). Phase 1 targets 180 examples (15 per axis across 4 axes, 3 examples each). Phase 2 targets 600+ examples for 50+ entries per axis with multi-topic coverage. At a realistic human-authoring rate of 2-3 examples per hour, Phase 1 alone represents 60-90 hours of writing.

LLM-generated examples carry a specific risk that is distinct from general quality concerns: model-monoculture prose. All examples generated by Claude, GPT, or Gemini at their default settings converge on a house style. That house style is competent, helpful, and clear. It is also recognizable, and it is the same regardless of which voice or tone is nominally being demonstrated. A "pragmatic-architect voice" example and a "warm-pastoral voice" example generated by the same model in the same session may differ in topic framing but share the same syntactic patterns, hedge frequency, and transition words.

A related risk is accumulated drift. Each new generation round that uses previous outputs as examples or few-shot prompts amplifies model-typical patterns. Over time the catalog becomes a record of what models produce, not a catalog of human voice diversity.

These risks require a quality gate between generation and promotion. The gate must specifically test voice fidelity - whether the example is identifiable as the named entry - not just general writing quality.

## Decision

LLM-generated examples follow a mandatory three-stage promotion path:

Stage 1 - Draft: Examples are generated with `author_type: llm`, `review_status: draft`, `llm_model`, and `llm_prompt_file` fields set. Draft examples are committed to the repository but are not indexed in the public catalog build.

Stage 2 - Reviewed: The maintainer reads the example and confirms voice fidelity before promoting to `review_status: reviewed`. Voice fidelity is defined as: a reader who knows the named voice/tone/format can identify which entry the example belongs to without seeing the frontmatter. "Nice writing" is not sufficient for promotion. The example must be specifically identifiable as the named entry.

Stage 3 - Stable: Promotion to `review_status: stable` requires the example reads as authentically rendered across at least two anchor topics. Reference-quality examples (used in the SDK tests and the catalog landing page) require a third review pass confirming they serve as strong positive training examples.

Examples that fail voice-fidelity review are either rewritten (`author_type: hybrid`, `review_status: draft`) or discarded. Discarded examples are deleted, not kept in a "rejected" state, to avoid polluting the repository with content that failed review.

The `llm_model` and `llm_prompt_file` fields are required for all LLM-generated examples and for the LLM-generation stage of hybrid examples.

## Consequences

### Positive
- The quality gate prevents LLM-monoculture examples from entering the stable catalog. Readers of stable examples will encounter voice-differentiated prose, not model-typical prose.
- The audit trail documents generation lineage for every example, enabling future analysis of which models and prompts produced the best voice-fidelity results.
- Hybrid authoring is explicitly supported and first-class. Human revision of model drafts is encouraged as a time-efficient path to high-quality examples.

### Negative
- Review is the bottleneck. The maintainer estimates 20 hours for the Phase 0 example set at thorough review pace (1 hour per example with side-by-side comparison against the ENTRY.md definition).
- Review quality degrades when the maintainer is the only reviewer. Single-reviewer bias means some poor examples may be promoted because the reviewer has lost calibration over time.
- The three-stage path adds process overhead for contributors who want to submit examples. They must understand the promotion stages and set the correct frontmatter fields.

### Neutral
- Community contribution in Phase 3 adds second-reviewer capacity. The review policy will be updated at Phase 3 to require two approvals for stable promotion.
- The policy does not prevent contributors from submitting human-authored examples directly at `author_type: human`, `review_status: reviewed`. Human-authored examples skip the draft stage and move directly to the reviewed stage pending maintainer sign-off.
